article_12 · record-keeping · the core fit
claim_art12 verifying

Orc satisfies Article 12's logging requirements by construction, not by configuration.

Article 12 requires automatic recording of events over the lifetime of the system, with logs that identify risk situations, support post-market monitoring, and monitor operation. Article 26(6) requires retention of at least six months. Orc's trace + replay subsystem is the machinery to meet these requirements. It cannot be disabled, runs by default on every invocation, and produces append-only artifacts.

art12_§1
auto log
Automatic recording of events over the lifetime.
Every CLI or MCP invocation opens a Run, which writes a row to the run table and a JSON file under traces/<YYYY>/<MM>/<run_id>.json. There is no flag to turn this off.
code · src/orc/runs/runner.py · evidence: every test fixture in tests/unit/test_run_context.py asserts trace creation

Verdict labels contradicted, not_found, and partial explicitly mark unreliable outputs. Runtime errors are recorded with status="error" and error_message. A run with status=error is, by definition, a risk-flag event.

article_13 · transparency to deployers
claim_art13 verifying

Every Orc output is interpretable, cited, and inspectable.

Article 13 requires sufficient transparency that deployers can interpret and appropriately use the output. Orc's verdict shape is structured JSON with confidence + chunk citations + reasoning, which is materially more interpretable than the conversational outputs typical of LLM tooling.

Every verdict ships with: structured label (supported / partial / contradicted / not_found), confidence score, reasoning text, chunk-level citations, the full retrieval set.

article_14 · human oversight · one item open
claim_art14 verifying

Single-approver human oversight is shipped. Multi-approver for Article 14 §5 is open.

Article 14 requires effective oversight by natural persons. For some Annex III systems (notably biometric ID under §1(a)) it requires two natural persons to verify identifications. The single-approver case is satisfied today by the approval queue. The two-approver workflow is on the Phase 2 roadmap and is honestly marked open below.

art14_§4d
override
Decide not to use, or override, the output.
orc approve reject <id> --note "..." records the override decision with timestamp and free-text reason. The rejection is preserved in the queue and the trace.

art14_§4e
intervene
Intervene or interrupt.
The approval queue is structurally between Orc's analysis and any external action. Action-taking outputs land in the queue; nothing leaves Orc until a human accepts. Compromised or malfunctioning runs cannot bypass this — there is no path that doesn't pass through the queue.
architecture · approval queue is the only sanctioned write path

art14_§5
two-person
Two natural persons verify (open · Phase 2).
Not yet shipped. Article 14 §5 requires that for some Annex III systems, no action is taken unless verified by two natural persons. The approval table currently accepts one decision. Phase 2 adds an approvers_required field plus a per-approver decision log. Tracked at github.com/Thormatt/orc.

scope · what orc is not · honest framing
claim_scope verifying

Orc is a tool that helps deployers meet obligations. It does not absolve them.

Orc itself is not a "high-risk AI system" per Annex III. It is a tool used by deployers and providers of high-risk systems. Article 12/13/14 obligations strictly fall on the deployer; Orc supplies the machinery to meet them. Buyer-side legal counsel should always confirm your specific obligations.

also out of scope, marked honestly Conformity assessment under Article 43 is a procedural obligation, not a tool output. SOC 2 Type II and ISO/IEC 42001 attestations are on the Phase 4 roadmap; Orc v0.1.0 is open-source, MIT-licensed, fully inspectable code — a different (and arguably stronger) form of trust than a certificate, but not equivalent. General-purpose AI obligations under Articles 51–56 apply to the underlying LLM provider (Anthropic, OpenAI via OpenRouter), not to Orc itself.
