02The problem

Every regulated industry buying AI runs into the same wall: the model's output is not auditable.

  • The generation tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Glean) produce answers but not artifacts.
  • The agent frameworks (LangChain, LlamaIndex, AutoGen) compose calls but treat verification as an add-on.
  • The observability platforms (LangSmith, Langfuse, Phoenix) record traces designed for debugging, not regulator handoff.
  • The faithfulness judges (Patronus, RAGAS, Vectara HHEM) score answers but don't produce the trail of what got verified against what.

The EU AI Act Articles 12, 13, 14 require automatic event logging, transparency to deployers, and human oversight with named-approver decisions. US frameworks (NIST AI RMF) and sector regulations (HIPAA, SR 11-7, EBA model-risk) are moving the same direction. Today's stack doesn't satisfy any of them out of the box.

03The product

Orc is a CLI + MCP server that sits between "the model returned a string" and "ship it to the caller." It enforces four invariants by construction:

01 · Citations

Every verdict cites chunk IDs that exist in the retrieval set. Hallucinated IDs are dropped before the verdict ships — and a supported label can no longer be returned with zero valid citations (downgrades to not_found, the dropped IDs land in the trace).

Measured: 0 fabricated IDs leaked in 300 adversarial injections.

02 · Replay

Every call writes a versioned trace JSON: retrieval set, every LLM call's token + cache metrics, structured output, frozen corpus version. orc replay <run_id> re-executes the exact decision deterministically.

Schema-validated. Six structural invariants checked per run; current build passes all.

03 · Audit bundle

orc audit export --include-evidence packs traces, runs, evidence files, workspace DB, multi-approver decisions, and sha256 of every file into a single hashed tar.gz. An external auditor extracts and runs offline — no access to your infra required.

EU AI Act Article 12 + 26(6) record-keeping satisfied.

04 · Approval gate

Any verdict that mutates an external system lands in an approval queue first. approvers_required=2 enforced at the SQL level for Article 14 §5 high-risk classifications. Every decision timestamped, reasoned, and bundle-exported.

Database constraint prevents the same person voting twice.

04How it works

One call, five lines, every artifact:

# Create a workspace and ingest evidence you own
$ orc workspace create research
$ orc ingest ./contracts -w research

# Verify a claim against the corpus
$ orc verify "FY2023 EBITDA margin was 18.4%" --domain FinanceBench

# → SUPPORTED  confidence=0.91
#   reasoning: Computed 1247/6783 = 0.184 = 18.4%, matches.
#   tool_calls: [{calculate: "1247/6783*100", result: "18.38"}]
#   run_id: 01KS0QT1MSV3NZA8ZSJK6VKJ7H

# Replay deterministically months later
$ orc replay 01KS0QT1MSV3NZA8ZSJK6VKJ7H

# Hand a regulator a self-contained tarball
$ orc audit export -w research --include-evidence -o /handoff.tar.gz

Also exposed as an MCP server for Claude Code / Codex / any MCP-compatible agent. orc mcp serve.

The buyer is anyone whose AI work product has to survive a second reviewer six months later.

Financial services

SR 11-7 model risk management

Banks running LLM-assisted credit memos, transaction monitoring narratives, or earnings-call analyses need the exact prompt, retrieval set, and verdict chain reproducible on an examiner's machine. Orc produces the artifact natively.

EU enterprises

EU AI Act Article 12, 13, 14

High-risk AI systems deployed in the EU (HR screening, credit decisioning, biometrics) require automatic event logging, transparency to deployers, and multi-approver human oversight. Orc's runtime invariants map 1:1 to the regulatory text.

Healthcare & life sciences

Clinical evidence retrieval, regulatory filings

Pharma teams summarizing clinical trial data or drafting regulatory submissions need every claim traced to the underlying study. Orc's covidQA F1 = 0.95 shows the prose-heavy verification path works on medical literature shapes.

Legal & professional services

Contract review, e-discovery, opinion drafting

Law firms running AI-assisted contract review or e-discovery on confidential corpora need the verification step to cite the exact paragraph, not "the document says." Orc's chunk citations + workspace isolation give them both.

Editorial & research

Long-form journalism, intelligence briefs

Newsrooms and intelligence shops fact-checking AI-drafted reporting need verdicts ("the AI claimed X, our archive says Y") with replayable evidence. Orc is daily-driver shaped for this.

Govtech & defense

FOIA, accountability tooling, public records

Agencies and oversight bodies running LLM analyses on public-records archives need an audit trail that survives FOIA requests and judicial review. Single-file, hash-verified bundles are exactly the right shape.

06The moat

The judge model is a knob. The runtime is the moat.

Orc's verification path uses verify_claim(model=...). Swap in Llama 3.3 70B, Lynx (Patronus's own OSS judge), Qwen 2.5 72B, or any OpenAI-compatible endpoint — every artifact above (citations, replay, audit bundle, multi-approver) is unchanged.

This is the part the post-hoc judges and the observability platforms can't ship. They optimized one piece. Orc optimized the contract.

Orc's verification scores F1 0.86 on HaluBench — above Patronus AI's Lynx-70B home-court F1 of 0.85 on the same benchmark — using a general-purpose Claude Sonnet 4.6 call (no fine-tuning) and shipping the four things post-hoc judges don't: chunk-level citations validated at runtime, deterministic replay against a frozen corpus snapshot, hashed audit-export bundles (optionally self-contained), and a runtime invariant that refuses to deliver impossible citations in the first place.

Cost structure

Per-verification cost at Sonnet 4.6 rates with prompt caching: ~$0.005–$0.015. At 10K verifications/day that's ~$36K/year; at 100K/day ~$365K/year. Crossover to self-hosted 70B (Llama / Lynx) hits at ~50K/day. Customers pick the judge based on volume and privacy posture; the runtime never changes.

07Why now

  • Regulatory inflection. EU AI Act in force, NIST AI RMF 1.0 final, sector-specific deployer rules landing across financial services and healthcare. Audit-grade artifacts moved from nice-to-have to procurement requirement in 2025–2026.
  • The agentic stack matured. MCP shipped; agent frameworks proliferated. Every framework treats verification as a downstream add-on — none of them produce the regulator handoff. Documented gap: LangChain's own GitHub has an open issue (#35357) requesting Article-12-compliant logging.
  • The faithfulness benchmark ladder is decided. Lynx-70B holds the top of the leaderboard at 0.85. Orc's runtime, with a general-purpose Sonnet call and a 200-line calculator, just landed at 0.86. The remaining F1 ceiling is bounded; the artifact moat is wide open.
  • Buyer mindset shifted. Q4 2025 forward, every CISO and risk officer is being asked the same question by their board: "show me what the model said, why it said it, and prove no one can change the answer after the fact." Orc is the shortest path to a credible answer.

08What's shipped vs what's next

Shipped (v0.1.4, today)

  • Five verification modes (evidence, judgment, binary, decomposed, arithmetic) with source-aware routing.
  • Multi-turn tool-use loop primitive — safe arithmetic evaluator, ready for additional tools (lookup, comparison, etc.).
  • Audit export with optional self-contained bundles for offline regulator handoff.
  • Multi-approver workflow with SQL-enforced uniqueness for EU AI Act Article 14 §5.
  • MCP server + CLI. Drop-in compatible with Claude Code, Codex, any MCP-enabled agent.
  • 188-test suite, deterministic replay, hashed manifests, zero-cost local development.

Roadmap

  • Hosted runtime with scheduled triggers, team workspaces, and a web dashboard for non-technical reviewers.
  • gads, legal, marketing directives on the same runtime — each one a config + skill package, not a new product.
  • Embedding-based retrieval (Voyage / local sentence-transformers) + hybrid RRF.
  • orc eval consistency | perturb | regression — reliability instrumentation for production deployments.
  • Customer pilots with EU-deployed financial-services and healthcare teams. Letters of intent welcomed; reach out.
